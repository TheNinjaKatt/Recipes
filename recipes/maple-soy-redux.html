Maple Soy Reduction Sauce

Chef Lester Lepiten

Description

This is a recipe I learned while working at a ski resort in Utah.

It's incredibly simple, using only 6 ingredients. A blender is recommended, however, isn't required if the solid ingredients are finely minced.

A rich sauce that pairs well with chicken, as well as other proteins, and rice. Be sure to experiment and discover more pairings as this is a versatile condiment.

Ingredients

  • Reduced sodium Soy Sauce
  • Real Maple Syrup (Syrup made with corn syrup, although cheaper, is unfit for this recipe as it burns easily. Trust.)
  • Ginger Root
  • Shallots
  • Green Onions
  • High-Temp Cooking Oil

Steps

  1. Chop (or mince, if not using a blender) peeled ginger, shallots, and green onions.
  2. Sautee solid ingredients with oil in a saucepan until aromatic.
  3. Add equal parts Maple Syrup and Soy Sauce to the saucepan.
  4. Bring mixture to a boil.
  5. Reduce to simmer until sauce is reduced by half.
  6. Remove from heat and let cool until warm.
  7. Puree in blender until smooth. (Unless solid ingredients were minced.)
  8. Pour into bottles/storage containers and let cool to room temperature before refrigerating.
